01 · Themes

Research interests

I work across several linked areas rather than one narrow topic: how people think, learn, experience mental health, and participate in research — and how research can be designed, delivered, synthesised, and communicated well.

Learning & development

Individual differences

My PhD uses adolescent additional-language learning as a way to understand why people differ in learning outcomes, and how cognitive, linguistic, educational, and environmental factors shape those differences.

Mental health

Clinical and digital psychology

My previous work has focused on psychosis, cognitive remediation, relapse prediction, digital mental health tools, and the acceptability of remote psychological interventions.

Research practice

Methods, synthesis, and delivery

I am interested in rigorous and usable research: systematic reviews, meta-analysis, qualitative interviewing, participant engagement, project coordination, and research that can be implemented beyond papers.

Working identity

Not just “language learning.”

My current PhD topic is language learning, but the broader thread in my work is psychological research that connects individual experience, evidence, and practice. I am especially interested in roles where I can combine research methods, coordination, participant-facing work, public involvement, and thoughtful dissemination.

02 · Projects

Selected projects

A small set of projects showing the range of my research experience, from adolescent learning to psychosis research, public involvement, digital health, and community-led language work.

PhD · University of Cambridge

Individual differences in adolescent language learning

Investigating internal and external predictors of additional-language attainment during adolescence, using evidence synthesis and empirical methods.

King’s College London

CONNECT Digital Study

Supported a digital study using devices such as smartphones and smartwatches to help predict relapse in psychosis, including participant engagement, assessments, check-ins, and relapse assessments.

Remote cognitive remediation

Acceptability of remote psychological intervention

Recruited participants, conducted semi-structured interviews, completed reflexive thematic analysis, led service-user advisory panels, and contributed to publication and dissemination.

Community & language

Welsh language and lived experience

Supported community-led and minority-language research, including work connected to Welsh language, belonging, dissemination, and lived-experience methods.

03 · Skills

What I bring to projects

This section is deliberately broader than a CV. It helps collaborators, supervisors, labs, and future employers quickly understand the kinds of work I can contribute to.

Research coordination

Participant recruitment, study administration, assessment scheduling, check-ins, documentation, adverse event reporting, and multi-stakeholder communication.

Evidence synthesis

Systematic reviewing, screening, extraction, risk of bias work, synthesis planning, meta-analysis, and translating scattered evidence into usable summaries.

Qualitative research

Semi-structured interviewing, reflexive thematic analysis, patient-centred research, cognitive debriefing, and concept elaboration.

Data and analysis

Quantitative analysis, data cleaning, review tools, research databases, and developing transparent workflows for complex datasets.

PPI and lived experience

Designing and facilitating advisory panels, integrating service-user perspectives, and developing research materials that are accessible and respectful.

Dissemination

Academic writing, non-academic communication, presentations, public-facing resources, visual/creative approaches, and bilingual communication.
